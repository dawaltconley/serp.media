## FAQ


### What is a record player?

A record player is an audio playback device designed to play vinyl records. It consists of a turntable, tonearm, and cartridge, which work together to convert the grooves on the vinyl record into sound waves that can be heard through speakers. 


### Why are record players popular again?

Record players are experiencing a resurgence in popularity due to several factors, including nostalgia for the classic sound of vinyl records, the growing interest in collecting physical music, and the trend of DJs and musicians incorporating turntables into their live performances.

* Auto-return tonearm for easy record storage

### How do I maintain my record player?

Proper maintenance of a record player includes keeping it clean, adjusting the tracking force, lubricating the tonearm and turntable bearings, and handling and storing records carefully. 


### What are some brands that produce affordable record players?

Some popular brands for affordable record players include Victrola, Naxos, and Jensen. 


### What is the difference between a record player and a turntable?

While the terms are often used interchangeably, a turntable is a component of a record player that rotates the vinyl record. A record player, on the other hand, is an entire system that includes the turntable, tonearm, cartridge, and amplification components necessary to play the vinyl record and produce sound.
