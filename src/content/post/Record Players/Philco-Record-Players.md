### Features to Consider


#### Turntable Construction

One of the most important aspects of a Philco record player is the turntable construction. The main types are direct drive and belt drive. Direct drive systems provide more consistent speed and are less susceptible to wear, making them a good choice for long-term use. Belt-drive systems, on the other hand, offer a smoother sound and are generally considered more enjoyable for casual listening.

#### Speed Settings

Most Philco record players come with 33 1/3 and 45 RPM speed settings, but some may also include a 78 RPM setting. Ensure the record player you choose has the appropriate speed settings for your collection. 


#### Sound Quality

Sound quality is a crucial factor when selecting a Philco record player. Look for models with high-quality components, such as tone arms with adjustable tracking force and cartridges with superior frequency response. #### Built-in Speakers

Some Philco record players come with built-in speakers, while others may require external speakers. Consider your listening preferences and the available space in your living area when deciding whether to choose a model with built-in or external speakers. 


#### Connectivity

Modern Philco record players may include connectivity options, such as Bluetooth or USB inputs, allowing you to play digital files directly from your smartphone or computer. If you plan on using these features, ensure the record player you choose supports them.
