## Buyer's Guide

Welcome to our Orbit Record Players buyer's guide! In this section, we'll discuss the key features to consider when purchasing a record player and provide general advice on how to get the most out of your vinyl listening experience. Remember, the goal is to find a high-quality record player that meets your specific needs and preferences. 


### Turntable Construction


The construction of the turntable is essential in determining the overall sound quality of the record player. There are two main types of turntable materials: plastic and metal. Plastic turntables are more affordable but can introduce more vibrations and noise to the sound. Metal turntables are heavier and provide better stability, reducing vibrations and resulting in cleaner, more accurate audio. 


### Speaker Quality

The built-in speakers on some record players might suffice for casual listening, but if you're looking for a superior listening experience, consider investing in a separate standalone speaker system or high-quality headphones. This will provide better sound quality, improved bass response, and a more immersive listening experience. 


### Connectivity Options

Consider your playback options and the connectivity features of the record player. If you want to stream music from your smartphone, tablet, or other devices, look for models with Bluetooth or Wi-Fi capabilities. Some record players also support line-in connections for external devices. Make sure the record player has the appropriate connectivity options for your needs. 



### Preamp Functionality

If you plan on connecting your turntable to a receiver or other audio equipment, ensure the record player has a built-in preamp. This will allow you to connect your turntable directly to the audio system, eliminating the need for a separate preamp or phono stage. 


### Maintenance and Care

Proper maintenance and care are essential for keeping your Orbit record player functioning optimally. Always store your record player in a cool, dry area to prevent damage from fluctuations in temperature and humidity. Keep the stylus clean and replace it regularly to maintain accurate tracking and prevent wear on the vinyl. Regularly clean your vinyl records to prevent dust and scratches from affecting the sound quality. 

Choosing the right Orbit record player is a matter of understanding your specific needs and preferences. By considering factors such as turntable construction, speaker quality, connectivity options, preamp functionality, and maintenance, you can make an informed decision and enjoy your vinyl collection to the fullest. Happy listening!
