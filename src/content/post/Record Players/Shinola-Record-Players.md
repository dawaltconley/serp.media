## FAQ



### What makes Shinola record players unique?

Shinola record players are unique because of their high-quality materials, craftsmanship, and attention to detail. These products are designed in Detroit and assembled by hand, with each component sourced from the United States. They feature brass components, premium wood, and steel frames, making them durable and aesthetically pleasing. ### What is the difference between manual and automatic record players?

Manual record players require the user to manually adjust the speed and tonearm to play the record, while automatic record players do these tasks automatically. Manual turntables offer greater control over the playback process but are more time-consuming if you play multiple records. Automatic turntables are more user-friendly and convenient, but may have limitations on speed settings or features. 



### How to connect a turntable to a speaker system?

* First, identify the correct connections for your turntable and speakers. Most turntables have RCA outputs, which need to be connected to the RCA inputs on your speakers or receiver.

* Make sure the turntable power cord is connected to a power outlet.

* Connect the RCA cables from the turntable to the inputs on your receiver or amplifier. Ensure that the colors of the cables match the corresponding inputs on the devices.

* Once all connections are made, turn on the turntable and receiver/amplifier, then carefully lower the tonearm onto the record.

* Start the record by moving the tonearm's lever to the start position or pressing the start button on the turntable.


### What are some common issues with record players and how can they be avoided?

Some common issues with record players include skips, scratches, and poor sound quality. Ensure that your records are stored properly in sleeves and jackets to prevent scratches. Clean your records and turntable thoroughly to prevent debris and dust from interfering with playback. Adjust the tonearm and platter speed to ensure smooth operation. 


### Do Shinola record players come with built-in speakers?


Most Shinola record players do not come with built-in speakers, but they typically feature high-quality exterior design and premium materials. They are designed to be a centerpiece of your home, often displaying their stunning craftsmanship. 


### Can the tonearm be adjusted for optimal tracking?

Yes, Shinola record players usually come with an adjustable tonearm to allow for optimal tracking for different records. The counterweight, tracking force, and anti-skating system also work together to ensure accurate and smooth playback. 


### What types of records can be played on Shinola record players?

Shinola record players are compatible with a variety of vinyl records including LPs (33 RPM), 45s (45 RPM), and 78s (78 RPM). However, it's always a good idea to verify the compatibility of each individual turntable before acquiring any specific records.
