## Buyer's Guide



### Important Features

When considering Sony microphones, there are several key features to look for: 

* Polar Patterns

1. Cardioid - Capturing sound primarily from the front while minimizing pickup from the sides and rear.

2. Omnidirectional - Picking up sound all around, ideal for capturing ambient noise or group discussions.

3. Bidirectional - Capturing sound primarily from the front and rear, while minimizing side pickup, ideal for two-person interviews or live performances.

* Frequency Response

1. Low Frequency

2. Mid Frequencies

3. High Frequencies

* Sensitivity

1. Decibol Voltage (dBV)

2. Milliwatt

* Self-Noise

1. Equivalent Input Noise (EIN)

2. Sensitivity to mechanical vibration and electromagnetic interference


### Considerations

Before purchasing a Sony microphone, consider the following: 

* Intended use

+ Recording music or vocals

+ Filming video content or podcasting

+ Capturing audio in live performances

* Compatibility with your devices (camera, mixer, or recorder)

* Phantom power availability (if required)

* Durability and build quality

* Warranty and customer support



### General Advice

To ensure the best sound quality and performance from your Sony microphone, remember to: 

* Use proper microphone placement

+ For vocals, place the microphone at a distance of about 6-12 inches from the mouth for optimal sound quality.

+ For instruments, position the microphone near the sound source and experiment with angles and distances to achieve the desired sound.

* Minimize background noise

+ Choose an appropriate room or recording environment

+ Properly sound treat the area, if necessary

* Ensure proper gain staging to prevent clipping or distortion

+ Start with the gain at its lowest setting and gradually increase until the desired level is achieved

+ Monitor the signal with headphones or studio monitors to ensure accurate sound representation
